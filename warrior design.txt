The combined Warrior class

I would break the complete class into six ability families:

Weapon Attacks
Mobility
Stagger / Physical Control
Defense
Combat Momentum
Rally / Leadership

Not every build uses all six.

1. Weapon Attacks

These are the core of the class.

Heavy Strike

The basic powerful weapon attack.

Deal heavy Strike damage to one target.

This is your basic class-defining attack. It can become:

Higher damage
Higher Stagger
Cleaving
Armor penetration
Bonus damage against Staggered enemies

This is the foundation of the GW1 attack-skill / Titan Quest Onslaught side of the class.

Cleave

Simple frontal or nearby AoE weapon attack.

Strike the target and nearby enemies.

This is the cleanest basic AoE tool.

It gives the warrior a useful answer to packs without requiring a complicated ability.

Whirlwind

Mobile or stationary spinning attack.

Repeatedly attack nearby enemies.

This is the obvious combined descendant of Titan Quest's War Wind and the broader warrior AoE tradition.

Possible directions:

Move while spinning
Build more Stagger
Deal more damage to a single target
Pull enemies inward
End with a powerful final strike
Crushing Blow

Large single-target hit.

Deal a powerful Strike attack with high Stagger damage.

This is the warrior's big "I want to create an opening" attack.

I think this should be one of the core Strike class abilities.

Execute

Finisher.

Deal increased damage to enemies below a health threshold or in a vulnerable state.

This is a classic warrior ability and gives the class a satisfying end to its combat loop.

The strongest version for your game may be:

Deals bonus damage to Staggered enemies.

That creates:

Build Stagger
    ↓
Enemy becomes Staggered
    ↓
Execute
2. Mobility

This is where the warrior gets much more interesting than a slow tank.

Charge

Primary gap closer.

Rush to an enemy and attack them.

This should almost certainly be in the class.

Possible variations:

Massive Stagger on impact
Area damage
Defensive buff after arrival
Offensive buff after arrival
Charge without a target

This combines the core ideas of Grim Dawn's Blitz and DOS2's Battering Ram-style mobility.

Leap

Jump to a location and attack.

Leap to an area, damaging and Staggering enemies on landing.

This is different enough from Charge to justify both:

Charge
Target-focused
Fast
Good for single-target engagement
Leap
Area-focused
More flexible
Good for jumping into or out of groups

This is the kind of skill I would definitely consider for your class.

Sprint

Short-term movement stance/buff.

Move faster for a duration.

This is a very simple ability, but GW1 demonstrates that a straightforward movement stance can be valuable as part of a martial kit.

You could morph it into:

Offensive movement
Defensive movement
Break free from movement impairments
Gain a buff when Sprint ends
3. Stagger / Physical Control

This is where your own system gives the warrior a particularly strong identity.

Ground Slam

Area Stagger tool.

Slam the ground, damaging nearby enemies and dealing heavy Stagger damage.

This should probably be one of the warrior's core skills.

It can become:

A cone
A circle
A shockwave
A single-target crushing blow
War Cry

Enemy-facing control ability.

Emit a powerful cry that affects nearby enemies.

Possible effects:

Increase Stagger damage taken
Reduce enemy damage
Interrupt
Apply a debuff
Force enemies to attack you
Briefly stagger weak enemies

This is the natural warrior counterpart to Rally.

Knockdown / Knockback

I would not necessarily make this a separate universal ability. Instead, several abilities can have it as a possible morph.

For example:

Ground Slam

One morph creates a short knockdown.

Charge

One morph knocks the target backward.

Crushing Blow

One morph causes a powerful stagger effect.

This is similar to how DOS2's Warfare identity revolves around physically controlling enemies through weapon attacks and knockdowns.

For your game, I would make Stagger the universal system and reserve stronger knockdowns/stuns for specific skills or morphs.

4. Defense

I think this is where we should be careful not to turn the class into “the tank class.”

The warrior should have good defensive tools, but they should be active and build-defining.

Brace / Guard

Basic defensive cooldown.

Temporarily reduce incoming damage.

Possible morphs:

Gain Block
Reduce damage more strongly
Reflect some damage
Gain offensive power when the effect ends
Protect nearby allies

This is the simple, reliable defensive button.

Counterguard

Reactive defense.

Enter a defensive stance. The next time you successfully defend, retaliate.

This is much more interesting than another generic damage reduction skill.

It can reward:

Reading the enemy and timing your defense.

Possible morphs:

Counterattack deals high Stagger
Counterattack hits nearby enemies
Successful counter grants a buff
Counter prevents a lethal hit
Last Stand

Emergency survival skill.

Prevent death or become extremely difficult to kill for a short duration.

This is the Grim Dawn Menhir's Will / Titan Quest defensive-survival tradition. Grim Dawn's Soldier explicitly combines high durability with active survival tools and leadership abilities.

Possible versions:

Cannot die for a short duration
Survive lethal damage with 1 HP
Gain defense based on missing health
Become immune to Stagger
Gain power as health decreases

I would definitely include some version of this eventually.

5. Combat Momentum

This is one of the biggest things I think your class should borrow from GW1 and Titan Quest.

The warrior should feel like it gets stronger by actively fighting.

You don't need a class-specific resource to do this.

Instead, use temporary buffs.

Onslaught

A stacking combat buff.

Successful weapon attacks build stacks of Onslaught, increasing your offensive power.

For example:

Attack
  ↓
Onslaught stack
  ↓
More damage / attack speed / Stagger
  ↓
Continue attacking

Titan Quest's Warfare mastery uses Onslaught as a central persistent attack mechanic, while GW1's Warrior uses adrenaline and attack skills to create combat momentum.

For your game, I think this is much better than a separate Warrior resource.

It is simply:

A buff that rewards staying engaged.

Possible morphs:

More damage
More attack speed
More Stagger
Lose stacks when you stop attacking
Consume stacks for a powerful attack
Battle Rage

Short-term offensive empowerment.

Become more powerful after taking or dealing damage.

This could be a simple self-buff that gives:

Damage
Crit
Haste
Stagger power

This is another classic warrior idea that fits very naturally.

6. Rally / Leadership

This is the area where I think your Warrior class can become more unique than a generic ARPG warrior.

Rally

Basic party/summon buff.

Empower nearby allies and summons.

Possible effects:

Damage
Defense
Haste
Stagger damage
Resource regeneration

This should be a core class ability.

Battle Standard

Area-based buff.

Place a banner that empowers allies within an area.

This is the stronger, more positional version of Rally.

Titan Quest's Battle Standard is one of the clearest examples of the warrior having a powerful battlefield leadership tool.

Possible morphs:

Offensive banner
Defensive banner
Stagger banner
Summon banner
Move the banner with you
Command

Directly empower allies or summons.

Grant a powerful temporary buff to a target ally or summon.

This is where the Warrior could become a genuinely good summon-support multiclass.

Examples:

Your summon deals increased damage
Your summon gains increased Stagger
Your summon cannot be interrupted
Your summon gains your Rally effects
Your summon immediately attacks a target

This is probably a later-game ability rather than a level 1–10 prototype skill.
